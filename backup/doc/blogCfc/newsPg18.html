Slideshow Support (First 5.2 Alpha checkin)

Aug 18

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 5 Comments

For 5.2 I decided to go with a super simple slide show support. Right now you simply just have to upload a folder to /images/slideshows, then to link to it, go to

yourblog.com/slideshow.cfm/foldername

To see an example of this, go here:

http://www.blogcfc.com/slideshow.cfm/test1

For 5.3 I'm going to build an admin tool for slide shows. It will let you:

  • See all slideshow folders
  • Delete an image, upload an image
  • Make a new slideshow folder
  • Write a slide show title and caption for images. This will be stored in an XML file as to not need another database change.

This is now checked into SVN and represents the first 5.2 Alpha release. I will be doing a bigger check in tonight.

Date Issues

Aug 15

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 11 Comments

Believe it or not, there are countries out there in the world who have decided to buck the norm and change what is commonly accepted as a good date format:

month/day/year

These liberal, God-hating countries have decided that it would be better to do:

day/month/year

Now - those of us in the good ole U S of A can do our best to ignore these freaks, but, it seems as if I have a few BlogCFC users from these poor, confused countries. I worked with one such person today. He had an issue where his dates that looked like 15/8/06 turned into dates in the year 2015. I made a fix for him, but I'd like it people (both Americans and the rest of the world) could please try this fix.

Go to your admin/entry.cfm file, find these lines:

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 <!--- Before we save, modify the posted time by -1 * posted --->
<cfset form.posted = dateAdd("h", -1 * application.blog.getProperty("offset"), form.posted)> 1<!--- Before we save, modify the posted time by -1 * posted --->
2<cfset form.posted = dateAdd("h", -1 * application.blog.getProperty("offset"), form.posted)>

Before them, insert:

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 <!--- convert datetime to native --->
<cfset form.posted = lsParseDateTime(form.posted)> 1<!--- convert datetime to native --->
2<cfset form.posted = lsParseDateTime(form.posted)>

Please tell me if this works for you. By the way, I have a high level of respect for my readers and users, but, just in case it isn't obvious, the whole introduction above was just a joke. I love the rest of the world. Really.

Fix Smart Quotes

Aug 7

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 0 Comments

Rob reminded me today I never got around to fixing smart quotes. That's the "feature" in MS Word (and even OpenOffice Writer) where things like quotes and hyphens are changed into funky characters.

These characters show up ok in the blog, but not in the RSS feed. (Which, by the way, is a bug in xmlFormat, as xmlFormat blissfully ignores them.)

I've included the fix in 5.2, but since that may be a ways out, here is a quick fix. It makes use of a line from Nathan Dintenfas' UDF, SafeText.

Here is the fix. This belongs in entry.cfm and I assume you can find the right position.

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 <cfset form.body = trim(form.body)>
        
<!--- Fix damn smart quotes. Thank you Microsoft! --->
<!--- This line taken from Nathan Dintenfas' SafeText UDF --->
<!--- www.cflib.org/udf.cfm/safetext --->
<cfset form.body = replaceList(form.body,chr(8216) & "," & chr(8217) & "," & chr(8220) & "," & chr(8221) & "," & chr(8212) & "," & chr(8213) & "," & chr(8230),"',',"","",--,--,...")> 1<cfset form.body = trim(form.body)>
2        
3<!--- Fix damn smart quotes. Thank you Microsoft! --->
4<!--- This line taken from Nathan Dintenfas' SafeText UDF --->
5<!--- www.cflib.org/udf.cfm/safetext --->
6<cfset form.body = replaceList(form.body,chr(8216) & "," & chr(8217) & "," & chr(8220) & "," & chr(8221) & "," & chr(8212) & "," & chr(8213) & "," & chr(8230),"',',"","",--,--,...")>

Follow up on new rendering engine

Aug 7

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 5 Comments

Want to include a CF file in a blog posting? Want it to be dynamic? Add this CFC to your render folder:

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 <cfcomponent extends="render" instructions="To include a file, use <cfinclude template=""xxx"" where xxx is relative to the blog CFC or specified with a ColdFusion Mapping.">

<cffunction name="display">
    <cfargument name="template" type="string" required="false" default="">
    <cfset var buffer = "">
    
    <cfif len(arguments.template)>
        <cfsavecontent variable="buffer">
        <cfinclude template="#arguments.template#">
        </cfsavecontent>
        <cfreturn buffer>
    </cfif>
    
    <cfreturn "">
    
</cffunction>

BlogCFC 5.2 Sneak Peak

Aug 4

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 4 Comments

The alpha is being worked on. One of the things I mentioned was the easy addition of tags to blogs. So, if you wanted <ray> to create a link to me, you could. If you wanted all your outgoing links to go out via a link counter and wanter an easy to do it, you could do <linktrack url="..."> - you can.

How hard is it going to be? Consider this CFC:

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 <cfcomponent extends="render" instructions="To place an left-aligned Amazon 'box' for a product, use: <amazonbox asin=""x"" affiliate=""y"">">

<cffunction name="display">
    <cfargument name="asin" type="string" required="false" default="">
    <cfargument name="affiliate" type="string" required="false" default="">
    
    <cfset var result = "">

    <cfif len(arguments.asin) and len(arguments.affiliate)>
        <cfsavecontent variable="result">
        <cfoutput>
I was called with asin=#arguments.asin# and affiliate=#affiliate#.
        </cfoutput>
        </cfsavecontent>
    </cfif>
        
    <cfreturn result>
</cffunction>

</cfcomponent> 1<cfcomponent extends="render" instructions="To place an left-aligned Amazon 'box' for a product, use: <amazonbox asin=""x"" affiliate=""y"">">
2
3<cffunction name="display">
4    <cfargument name="asin" type="string" required="false" default="">
5    <cfargument name="affiliate" type="string" required="false" default="">
6    
7    <cfset var result = "">
8
9    <cfif len(arguments.asin) and len(arguments.affiliate)>
10        <cfsavecontent variable="result">
11        <cfoutput>
12I was called with asin=#arguments.asin# and affiliate=#affiliate#.

14        </cfsavecontent>
15    </cfif>
16        
17    <cfreturn result>
18</cffunction>
19
20</cfcomponent>

Every entry will call the display method. (Technically it calls render, but more on that later.) Display will look for any token with the same name as your CFC. Every instead of that token will be passed to the display method along with the arguments. So if this CFC was named amazonBox, I can type this into a blog entry:

view plain copy to clipboard print about
  ColdFISH is developed by Jason Delmore. Source code and license information available at coldfish.riaforge.org
 Foo, moo, <amazonbox asin="..." />, and <amazonbox asin="moon" affiliate="..">. 1Foo, moo, <amazonbox asin="..." />, and <amazonbox asin="moon" affiliate="..">.

That's it. Validation is up to you. My code above wants both asin and affiliate, but you can do whatever rules/defaults you want.

BlogCFC 5.1.004 Released

Aug 1

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 5 Comments
