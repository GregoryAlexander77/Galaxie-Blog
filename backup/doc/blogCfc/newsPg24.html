Dude, where is the beta?

Apr 28

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 1 Comments

Sorry folks. I was out all day yesterday with... something. I call it the Evil Cough of Death. I'm still feeling poorly but not quite as bad today.

I was really close to releasing the beta when someone pointed out a simple category issue. This then led to another category issue, and then another, etc.

So I'm doing a bit of cleanup in the categories area, but I should wrap this today.

There will be a change to how category SES urls work for those of you upgrading from BlogCFC 4. They will NOT work out of the box. In order for them to work, you will simply need to go to each category in the admin and click save. Basically, I'm adding a category alias field to the db that is used for SES urls. In the admin, you have the option of making your own category alias, or just letting BlogCFC make it. So for your old data, you will do a one time process of click to edit, save, repeat, for all your categories.

I could make a script for this, but I figure even if you have twenty categories or so it will just take about a minute.

More on Related Entries

Apr 25

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 0 Comments

I forgot to mention. By default, related entries are bidirectional. They go both ways. However, Jeff/CJ added support to make them one way. While I don't plan on exposing it as a setting, you could easily tweak the code to make related entries one way.

Another tweak is the ability to hide a link to an entry in the future. This would allow you to stop old entries from showing links to new entries. Again, this is turned off, but if you wanted this, you could tweak it.

Both very cool options and really enhance the power of the feature.

Major New Feature

Apr 25

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 4 Comments

Another new major feature was added today - Related Entries. Related entries lets you link one blog entry directly to another. Think of it as a closer relationship than categories.

This feature was written by Jeff Coughlin and Charlie Griefer. All I did was integrate it. So big thanks to those two guys for adding a way cool feature. (You will not see credit given to them in the documentation as I haven't gotten that far yet.)

I'm now at Alpha 2. I think I'm ready to mark this baby "Beta". I just want to update the docs. Beta does not mean the end of new features, but I do think I've hit some of the big things I wanted done. When I go beta I'll be updating my main blog as well (eating my own dogfood).
