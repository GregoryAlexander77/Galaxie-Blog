5.5.003 in SVN

Oct 31

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 5 Comments

5.5.003 is in SVN. I'm going to blog this on the main blog as well (since there is an important new feature), but that won't be till tonight, so feel free to grab from SVN now.

Updates include:

  • Captivate support. You can now blog with Captivate.
  • When editing an entry, you can decide to NOT send an email to subscribers. This was mainly done for Captivate as it creates, and quickly deletes, a blog entry in order to create a design template. You can't make this decision for old blog entries though.
  • Fixed a bug that occurred if you were writing a blog entry that had an enclosure and your session timed out.
  • Blog owners won't get emails about form.captcha not existing. This was happening because of Spammer "Auto Attack" type scripts.

There are a few more small fixes as well in the readme.txt file.

For the next point release, I'm thinking of changing the entry edit form. I'm considering using DHTML tabs to hide the stuff most folks don't use. I'd love to see JUST title, body, and category, and nothing else. Speaking for myself, that is all I need 99% of the time. So if I can hide the rest with tabs or an accordion UI, then it would make blog editing a bit simpler.

Warning to folks about render support

Oct 11

posted by Raymond Camden in BlogCFC | 0 Comments

Remember that cool render support I added to BlogCFC 5.5 and didn't bother to really document? (Documentation is for wimps!) It is biting a few people in the rear - specifically folks who have this in their entries:

<include ... >

Since include is one of the render functions I shipped. To get rid of it, simply find the include.cfc and rename it.
